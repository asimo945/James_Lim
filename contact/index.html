About.

Hello, I am James, welcome!

I am a physicist and material scientist, currently a researcher in Applied Nano Technology Science (ANTS) in Taiwan. My main role in the company is to strategize the impact of new technology on the market. Meanwhile, I also help the engineering teams in application development and providing technical suggestions. Therefore, I spent most of my time in analyzing and interpreting experimental results. Occasionally, I join the research and development process for new metrology system, particularly in drafting code for identifying micor /nano structural features.

In my spare time, I enjoy to hiking, driving, and playing soccer or Gaelic football.

I earned my PhD in material science from University of Leeds in October 2018, where I worked in the Leeds electron microscopy and spectroscopy Centre (LEMAS) and collaborate with National Physical Laboratory (NPL) to develop a characterization protocol for heterogeneous graphene. In my dissertation, I cross-correlate the electron and photon-probe methods on graphene’s nanostructure features, so that the features can be quantified rapidly.

After I finished my PhD, I came back to Taiwan as a postdoctoral researcher in Academia Sinica. In the devision of applied science, we aimed to develop a functional surface structure to form plasmon and nanofocusing, specifically for the application of biosensing and Surface Enhance Raman Spectroscopy (SERS).

In 2020, I have decided to switch to work in the industry, so I joined the RAISE program at National Central University, and then work as a research fellow in ANTS.

During my resarch in graduate school, I develope interests in using data outside of nature science. The ability of visualizing, excloring, modeling, and somehow making predictions. I mostly work in matlab or python, but I’m always learning new languages and technologies.
